# Content Strategy & UX Writing Reference (2026)

## Tone of Voice Framework

### Context-Dependent Tone Switching
The spy/operative motif wraps academic tasks in RPG narrative. But tone must shift based on context:

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Onboarding & Quest Briefings | Enthusiastic, Thematic, Mysterious | "Welcome to the Agency. Your first directive is to decrypt the kinematic equations outlined in the primary dossier." |
| Academic Rubrics & Grading | Formal, Objective, Encouraging | "Approaching Proficiency: The calculations demonstrate partial understanding, but require further refinement in vector isolation." |
| Error States & Submission Failures | Direct, Helpful, Non-punitive | "Network error. The assignment could not be uploaded. Please verify your connection and attempt the submission again." |
| Loot Drops & Achievements | Celebratory, Exaggerated, Rewarding | "Artifact Secured! You have obtained the Quantum Deflector (Legendary), granting a 5% boost to daily Cyber-Flux yields." |

### Critical Rule
**Never gamify error states or academic warnings.** When a student's submission fails, when they're flagged for integrity, or when they receive a low rubric score — drop the RPG persona entirely. Use formal, clear, compassionate language.

### Success States — Lean Into the Lore
Level-ups, item drops, achievement unlocks, boss defeats — these are the dopamine moments. Use dynamic, varied microcopy that:
- References the spy/operative narrative
- Rewards the user emotionally
- Reinforces the engagement feedback loop
- Varies to avoid repetition (maintain a pool of variant messages)

## Inclusive Narrative Design

### Themes to Embrace
- Espionage and intelligence gathering
- Puzzle-solving and cryptography
- Collaborative investigation
- Data analysis and code-breaking
- Sleek cyberpunk aesthetics

### Themes to Avoid
- Hyper-masculine or aggressive militaristic language
- Violent combat descriptions (frame boss encounters as "challenges" or "operations")
- Cultural stereotypes in character descriptions
- Gendered language in system messages
- Exclusionary insider jargon that alienates newcomers

### Visual Aesthetic Direction
When generating images via /generate-image or designing UI elements:
- Sleek, neon-tinged cyberpunk visuals
- Cryptographic interfaces and data streams
- Abstract data visualizations
- Holographic UI elements
- NO literal weapons, combat violence, or military imagery

## ISLE Pedagogy Framing

When the /lesson-plan skill generates content or the UX writer reviews lesson blocks:

### ISLE Sequence as RPG Narrative
| ISLE Phase | RPG Framing | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| Observe | Reconnaissance Mission | "Observe the footage and document anomalies in the classified recording." |
| Model | Intelligence Briefing | "The Agency's analysts have modeled the following relationship..." |
| Test | Field Operation | "Deploy to the simulation lab and verify the intelligence." |
| Apply | Mission Execution | "Apply your training to decode the real-world scenario." |

### Rubric Language (5-Level Scale)
Always use this exact scale with academic (not gamified) language:

| Level | Label | Tone |
|-------|-------|------|
| 1 | Missing | Neutral — "Evidence of the skill is not yet present." |
| 2 | Emerging | Encouraging — "Initial understanding is visible with significant gaps." |
| 3 | Approaching | Constructive — "Partial mastery demonstrated; specific areas need development." |
| 4 | Developing | Positive — "Strong understanding with minor refinements needed." |
| 5 | Refining | Celebratory — "Comprehensive mastery with sophisticated application." |

## Microcopy Pools

### Achievement Unlocked (vary these)
- "Intel Verified. New clearance level granted."
- "Operation complete. Your handler is impressed."
- "Decryption successful. New capabilities unlocked."
- "Field assessment passed. Advancing to next tier."

### Boss Defeated
- "Target neutralized. Intelligence recovered."
- "Operation concluded. The Agency sends their regards."
- "Encounter resolved. Analyzing recovered data..."

### Item Acquired
- "Artifact secured and cataloged."
- "New equipment requisitioned from R&D."
- "Field asset recovered. Integration complete."

### Daily Login
- "Agent reporting for duty. Daily briefing available."
- "Welcome back, operative. New directives awaiting."
- "Secure channel established. Ready for deployment."

## Writing Checklist for Content Strategist
1. Does the copy match the context tone (see framework above)?
2. Is academic content never obscured by the RPG narrative?
3. Are error messages clear without game language?
4. Does achievement copy vary (not the same message every time)?
5. Is the language inclusive and free of aggressive/violent framing?
6. Do rubric descriptions use formal academic language?
7. Is the spy motif consistent but not overwhelming?
