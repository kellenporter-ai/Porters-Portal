Willis Tower — Exemplar Solution

AP Physics 1 · Chapter 10: Vibrational Motion · Algebra-Based

GIVEN

Mass of Willis Tower: m = 2 × 10⁸ kg
Sway frequency: f = 0.10 Hz
Wind force (Part b): F = 4 × 10⁶ N
PART A: Effective Spring Constant
f = (1/2π)√(k/m)
Rearrange: k = (2πf)² · m
Substitute: k = (2π × 0.10)² × (2 × 10⁸) = (0.628)² × (2 × 10⁸) = 0.395 × 2 × 10⁸
ANSWER k ≈ 8 × 10⁷ N/m
Sig figs note: Mass = 1 sig fig (limiting), frequency = 2 sig figs → answer to 1 sig fig
PART B: Wind Displacement
F = kx → x = F/k
Substitute: x = (4 × 10⁶) / (8 × 10⁷)
ANSWER x ≈ 0.05 m = 5 cm
Sig figs note: F and k both have 1 sig fig → answer to 1 sig fig

ASSUMPTIONS

1. Restoring force is linear — Hooke's Law applies
2. Wind force treated as static load, not oscillating
3. Total mass treated as a lumped point mass
4. Damping is negligible
5. Small-angle sway approximation holds

REASONABLENESS CHECK

x/h = 0.05 m / 442 m ≈ 0.01% of tower height
Conclusion: 5 cm displacement is physically plausible for a stiff steel skyscraper.

NOTE

If students used k = 7.9 × 10⁷ N/m (less rounded), x = 0.051 m ≈ 5 cm — same result at 1 sig fig. Both values acceptable.
