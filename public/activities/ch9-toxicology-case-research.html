Real-World Environmental Poison Case Research

Research a real case of environmental poisoning, then use the guiding questions below to produce a forensic case brief you can submit to Google Classroom.

Instructions / Instrucciones

Your task: Research a real environmental poisoning case. Then answer the guiding questions in each section below to build a complete forensic case brief. This brief is what you will submit as your evidence.

Your job: find a real story about people getting poisoned by something in the environment (like lead in water, bad air, or chemicals). Then answer the questions below to write your report. Turn in this report for a grade.
  • Work with your group to research, but each student submits their own written brief.
  • Use your phone, Chromebook, or AI tools to find a legitimate case.
  • Go past Wikipedia — look for news articles, EPA reports, CDC case studies, or court documents.
  • Cite your sources at the end.

Not sure where to start? Open the AI Prompt box below for a copy-paste prompt you can use with any AI chatbot.

Copy and paste this into ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, or any AI you use:

I am a high school forensic science student. Help me find a real environmental poisoning case involving lead, mercury, carbon monoxide, or pesticides. I need to know: 1. What happened — the timeline and location of the exposure 2. Who was exposed and how many people were affected 3. What substance was involved and its properties 4. How forensic toxicologists detected or confirmed the substance 5. What health effects occurred (short-term and long-term) 6. What the legal or social outcome was (lawsuits, policy changes, settlements) 7. Whether this was an acute or chronic exposure case Please recommend one well-documented case with reliable sources (news, EPA, CDC, or peer-reviewed articles). Include links or source names if possible.
